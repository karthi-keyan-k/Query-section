Frequently Asked Questions

Why is the moon sometimes out during the day?
The moon is reflecting the sun's light. It doesn't matter where the Earth is in its rotation around the sun. The moon doesn't emit any light of its own.
Why is the sky blue?
When sunlight enters the Earth's atmosphere, it is scattered in all directions by the tiny molecules of gases such as nitrogen and oxygen.
Will we ever discover aliens?
The possibility of discovering aliens is a topic of ongoing research and debate in the fields of astrobiology, astrophysics, and the search for extraterrestrial intelligence (SETI).
How much does the Earth weigh?
The Earth's mass is approximately 5.97 x 10^24 kilograms.
How do airplanes stay up?
Airplanes stay up due to the principles of aerodynamics, which involve the shape of the wings and the movement of air around them.
